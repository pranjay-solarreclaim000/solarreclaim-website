# Qualification & Closing SOP (NEPQ Engine)

## 🎯 Objective
Transition a lead from "curious" to "committed" by utilizing Neuro-Emotional Persuasion Questioning (NEPQ). The goal is not to "sell" the service, but to help the prospect realize the gap between their current situation and their desired state, positioning SolarReclaim as the logical bridge.

## 🧠 The NEPQ Framework
Unlike traditional checklists, NEPQ focuses on psychological triggers, tonal shifts, and the "cost of inaction."

### 1. Connecting Phase
**Goal:** Lower resistance and establish a "neutral" frame. Avoid the "overly excited salesperson" persona.

- **Tonal Shift:** Use a calm, curious, and slightly detached tone.
- **Linguistic Markers:** "I'm not quite sure if we can even help you yet, but..."
- **Power Questions:**
    - "I saw you've been doing [X] for a while—just so I have some context, what actually led you to look into lead reactivation right now?"
    - "Just so I don't waste your time, would it be okay if I asked a few questions to see if this is even a fit for your current setup?"

### 2. Situation Phase
**Goal:** Understand the current state without triggering "defense mode."

- **Tonal Shift:** Fact-finding, objective.
- **Focus:** The lead database and previous attempts.
- **Power Questions:**
    - "How are you currently handling your dead lead database?"
    - "When was the last time you actually tried to reach back out to those old leads?"
    - "What's the current process for following up when a lead goes cold?"

### 3. Problem Phase
**Goal:** Identify the pain and the "cost of inaction." Move from surface-level problems to emotional impact.

- **Tonal Shift:** Empathy, concern, "discovering" the pain.
- **The Gap:** Highlight the difference between their current revenue and their potential revenue.
- **Power Questions (Pain & Implication):**
    - "You mentioned you have [X] dead leads... how is that actually affecting your growth right now?"
    - "If you don't find a way to reactivate these leads, what happens to your targets for the next quarter?"
    - "Does it feel like you're leaving a significant amount of money on the table every month by just letting these leads sit there?"
    - "How long has this been an issue for you?" (The "Duration" question to increase urgency).

### 4. Solution Phase
**Goal:** Position the reactivation service as the logical answer to the problem they just admitted to.

- **Tonal Shift:** Confident but not pushy; "expert advisor."
- **Linguistic Markers:** "Based on what you told me..."
- **Power Questions:**
    - "If we could essentially 'wake up' those dead leads and put qualified appointments on your calendar without you touching the phone, would that actually solve the gap we talked about?"
    - "What would it mean for your business if you could consistently add 5-10 more installs a month just from the data you already own?"

### 5. Commitment Phase
**Goal:** Secure the appointment and transition to the calendar.

- **Tonal Shift:** Decisive, guiding.
- **Power Questions:**
    - "It sounds like this is exactly what you're looking for. Based on everything we discussed, does it make sense to get a time locked in to map out the actual execution plan?"
    - "Do you have your calendar handy, or should I suggest a couple of times?"

---

## 📅 Scheduling Protocol
Once commitment is secured, the transition to the calendar must be seamless to prevent "buyer's remorse" or friction.

1. **Immediate Sync:** Do not say "I'll email you a link." Say, "Let's look at the calendar right now so we don't lose the slot."
2. **Double-Confirmation:** Confirm the date, time, and timezone.
3. **The "Value Anchor" Reminder:** While booking, remind them: "I've noted that we're specifically focusing on [Problem X] during the call so we can get those leads moving."
4. **Calendar Invitation:** Send the invite immediately after the call ends.
5. **Confirmation Message:** Send a brief SMS/Email confirming the appointment with a "What to expect" bullet point.

# Strategic Planning: CEO Pillar

## 1. The Mandate
The CEO Pillar owns the overarching strategic direction, vision, and high-level resource allocation of SolarReclaim. Its primary responsibility is to ensure the company is moving toward its North Star metrics while maintaining the integrity of the Growth Flywheel.

**Boundaries:**
- **Strategic Planning (CEO):** Defines *what* the goal is, *why* it is the goal, and *which* levers to pull.
- **Operational Execution (Business/Customer/Product):** Defines *how* the goal is achieved, the specific workflows, and the day-to-day management.
- The CEO Pillar provides the "Strategic Intent"; the other pillars provide the "Execution Result."

## 2. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
The North Star metrics for the CEO Pillar are:
- **Partner Growth:** Number of high-quality US Solar Installers (TX & FL) onboarded that meet the ICP (8-20 installs/mo, 4.2+ Google rating).
- **Revenue (Performance-Based):** Total revenue generated from shown appointments ($75/appt) and closed deal commissions (3-5%).
- **Conversion Efficiency:** The ratio of reactivated dead leads to shown appointments.

## 3. The Resource Map
### Tools
- **Strategic Dashboard:** High-level KPI tracking (e.g., Google Sheets, Notion, or custom dashboard).
- **Market Intelligence Feed:** Tools for monitoring solar industry trends and regulatory changes in TX/FL.
- **Decision Log:** The `1-system/1-ceo-pillar/memory.md` file for tracking strategic pivots.

### Roles
- **Founder/CEO:** Sole arbiter of strategic direction and final approval on client-facing communications.
- **Strategic Advisors (Future):** External experts for market scaling and legal/compliance oversight.

## 4. The Interface
Strategic decisions flow from the CEO Pillar into the other pillars as follows:
- **$\rightarrow$ Customer Pillar:** Defines the ICP and the "Zero Risk/Found Money" positioning for partner acquisition.
- **$\rightarrow$ Product Pillar:** Sets the quality standards for the "Lead Factory" and the desired conversion benchmarks.
- **$\rightarrow$ Business Pillar:** Sets the budget for tool procurement and the targets for revenue capture.

## 5. The Memory Link
All strategic state, decision logs, and KPI snapshots are tracked in:
`1-system/1-ceo-pillar/memory.md`

## 6. The Decision Framework
Rules for evaluating new partners or strategic opportunities:
1. **ICP Alignment:** Does the partner meet the strict criteria (8-20 installs/mo, 4.2+ rating, TX/FL)?
2. **Lead Database Quality:** Is the "dead lead" database sufficient in size and recency to justify the effort?
3. **Resource Impact:** Does this opportunity distract from the core Growth Flywheel or accelerate it?
4. **Risk Profile:** Does the partnership introduce legal or reputational risk (TCPA compliance, brand alignment)?

## 7. The Growth Flywheel
The SolarReclaim engine operates on a continuous loop:
**Partner Acquisition** $\rightarrow$ **Lead Reactivation** $\rightarrow$ **Revenue Generation** $\rightarrow$ **Profit Reinvestment** $\rightarrow$ **(Back to Partner Acquisition)**

- **Acquisition:** Attracting high-intent solar installers.
- **Reactivation:** Using the "Product Pillar" to turn dead leads into appointments.
- **Revenue:** Capturing performance fees and commissions.
- **Reinvestment:** Using profits to upgrade tooling, optimize scripts, and scale outreach.

## 8. Strategic Cadence
SolarReclaim utilizes a hybrid system to balance agility with long-term vision:

### Weekly Tactical Adjustments
- **Focus:** Short-term steering and "Course Correction."
- **Activity:** Review weekly KPI snapshots, analyze script A/B test results, and adjust outbound targeting.
- **Goal:** Maintain momentum and fix immediate bottlenecks.

### Quarterly Strategic Deep-Dives
- **Focus:** Structural alignment and "Flywheel Optimization."
- **Activity:** Comprehensive review of North Star metrics, updating the Decision Framework, and re-evaluating the ICP.
- **Goal:** Ensure the business is scaling sustainably and the mandate remains relevant.
